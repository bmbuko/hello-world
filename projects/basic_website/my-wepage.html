Castle of Goodhope

Built by the DEIC between 1666 and 1679, the Castle of Good Hope is the oldest existing colonial building in South Africa replaced an older fort called the Fort de Goede Hoop which was constructed from clay and timber and built by Jan van Riebeeck, the first Commander of the Cape. Its position marks the original shoreline before years of land reclamation changed the Table Bay coastline.
